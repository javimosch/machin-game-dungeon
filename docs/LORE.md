# LORE — The Fractured Dominion

*Setting, history, factions, and cosmology of the world inside `machin-game-dungeon`.*

---

## The World: Aethros

Aethros is a world in twilight. The old empires have crumbled, the ancient magics are fading, and what remains is a patchwork of petty kingdoms, hidden sanctuaries, and sunken ruins. The player is an adventurer — a nobody — who enters this world not to save it, but to survive it.

### The Fracture

Five hundred years ago, the **Aetherian Empire** ruled the known world from the White City of **Kal-Thorim**. Its mage-kings commanded the elements, its legions marched from sea to sea, and its spires touched the clouds. Then came the **Fracture** — a cataclysm of unknown origin that shattered the empire in a single night.

Theories abound:
- A failed ritual to reach the heavens tore reality apart
- The gods abandoned Aethros, and the empire's power collapsed
- A hidden enemy from the deep earth rose and consumed them from below
- The empire's own hubris — magic itself backfired and unmade them

No one knows the truth. What remains is the **Shattered Coast** — the western edge of the old empire, now a fragmented landscape of dungeons, ruins, monster-haunted wilderness, and isolated settlements.

### The Shattered Coast — Present Day

The year is 505 AF (After Fracture). The Shattered Coast is a region roughly 200 km × 200 km, bounded by:

- **The Serpent Sea** to the west — a treacherous ocean dotted with islands and sea-monster lairs
- **The Iron Mountains** to the east — a jagged range rich in ore, claimed by goblin clans
- **The Whisperwood** to the south — an ancient forest that seems to move when you aren't looking
- **The Grim Wastes** to the north — a barren tundra where the Fracture's scars are still visible

### Factions

#### The Free Cities
Three independent city-states that survived the Fracture:

| City | Ruler | Character | Key Feature |
|------|-------|-----------|-------------|
| **Havenmoor** | Elected Council | The last bastion of law | Stone walls, trade hub, fighter's guild |
| **Duskhollow** | The Nightfather (assassin lord) | Shadowy, mercantile | Black market, thieves' guild, safe house for fugitives |
| **Brightforge** | Master Smith Therin Ironhand | Gnomish/dwarven engineering | Underground forge-city, steam-powered machinery |

### The Dungeons

Dungeons in Aethros are not just monster lairs — they are **wounds in the world**. Each was created or shaped by the Fracture:

| Type | Origin | Characteristics |
|------|--------|-----------------|
| **Crypts** | Ancient burial sites | Cramped tombs, undead, trapped passages |
| **Mines** | Empire-era excavations | Vertical shafts, cave-ins, deep-earth monsters |
| **Temples** | Pre-Fracture holy sites | Tiled floors, statues, puzzles, guardians |
| **Ruins** | Fallen empire structures | Exposed to sky, crumbling walls, roof-open |
| **Caves** | Natural formations | Unpredictable layout, underground rivers, bioluminescence |
| **Shrines** | Post-Fracture cult sites | Recently built, traps, fanatics |

### The Player

You are an **adventurer** — a drifter who arrived in the Shattered Coast seeking fortune, escape, or both. You have no name, no past, no faction. Your story begins when you walk into your first dungeon.

The world does not care about you. It does not have a prophecy for you. You will earn every coin, every level, and every step of progress by what you find in the dark.

### Magic

Magic in Aethros is **vanishing**. Before the Fracture, mages shaped reality with words of power. Now, magic leaks from the world slowly — like water through a cracked bowl. What remains is:

- **Ember magic** — the most common, fire/light based. Found in torches, candles, simple cantrips.
- **Frost magic** — rare, associated with deep caves and the Grim Wastes.
- **Void magic** — forbidden. Taps into the Fracture itself. Powerful but corrupting.
- **Wild magic** — druidic, drawn from nature. Unpredictable but sustainable.

Spellcasting is not an academic pursuit — it is a **bargain**. Every spell draws on the dwindling reserves of the world. Some say magic will be gone entirely within a century.

### Religion

The old pantheon is silent. The Fracture is believed to have severed the connection between Aethros and its gods. What remains:

| Belief | Followers | Practice |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| **The Watcher** | Common folk | A nameless god who observes but does not intervene. Prayers are personal. |
| **Ancestor veneration** | Dwarven/goblin cultures | The dead watch over the living. Shrines in homes. |
| **The Fracture cults** | Desperate, mad | Worship the Fracture itself. Seek to reopen it. Dangerous. |
| **Silent ones** | Monastic | Meditation, isolation. Believe the gods will return if the world proves worthy. |
| **Nature spirits** | Rural, druidic | Local spirits of forests, rivers, mountains. Offerings for safe passage. |

The creatures of Aethros are not random — they are **ecosystem-driven**:

| Habitat | Common Creatures | Threat |
|---------|-----------------|--------|
| Dungeon crypts | Skeleton, zombie, wraith | Undead, slow but relentless |
| Deep caves | Giant rat, cave spider, troglodyte | Swarm tactics, ambush predators |
| Mines | Golem shard, crystal elemental, gas spore | Elemental, environmental hazards |
| Ruins | Ghost, animated armor, rust monster | Guardian constructs, territorial |
| Forests | Wolf, bear, giant boar, treant | Wildlife, territorial |
| Mountains | Goblin, harpy, cave troll | Tribal, organized raids |
| Coastal | Skeleton pirate, cliff strider, sea serpent | Territorial, ambush |
| Wastes | Frost wraith, ice golem, yeti | Isolation, extreme environment |
| Any (night) | Wraith, shadow, will-o'-wisp | Ethereal, drawn to living essence |

### The Dungeon Bloom

Once a year, on the **Night of the Weeping Moon**, new dungeon entrances appear across the Shattered Coast. No one knows why. Existing dungeons grow deeper, their layouts shifting. Monsters become more aggressive. The phenomenon is called the **Dungeon Bloom**.

The Blooms are why adventurers exist. Without them, the monsters would spill out across the land unchecked. Every adventurer who enters a dungeon and survives is holding the tide back — whether they know it or not.
