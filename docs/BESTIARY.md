## Classification

Creatures are categorized by **habitat** and **threat level**. Threat levels:

| Level | Color | Description | Player Level |
|-------|-------|-------------|-------------|
| ⚫ Trivial | White | Harmless, flee | 1 |
| ⚪ Minor | Green | Easy kill, low XP | 1-2 |
| ⚡ Moderate | Yellow | Fair fight | 2-4 |
| ⚠️ Dangerous | Orange | Hard fight, consider retreat | 4-6 |
| ☠️ Deadly | Red | Boss-level, prepare thoroughly | 6+ |
| 💀 Extreme | Purple | Overworld boss, group recommended | 8+ |

## Creature AI Design

### Behavior States

Each creature has a simple state machine:

```
IDLE ──(player in detection range)──→ ALERT
ALERT ──(player in chase range)──→ CHASE
CHASE ──(adjacent to player)──→ ATTACK
ATTACK ──(player moves away)──→ CHASE
CHASE ──(player out of range)──→ ALERT
ALERT ──(player leaves detection range)──→ IDLE
ATTACK ──(HP <= 0)──→ DEATH
```

### Detection Ranges

| Creature Type | Detection | Chase | Notes |
|---------------|-----------|-------|-------|
| Undead | 6 tiles | 10 tiles | Never gives up once alerted |
| Animal | 4 tiles | 8 tiles | Gives up if player leaves territory |
| Goblin | 6 tiles | 12 tiles | Calls reinforcements on alert |
| Boss | 10 tiles | Full dungeon | Cannot flee, must fight |

### AI Parameters

| Parameter | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| `detect_range` | Distance at which creature notices player |
| `chase_speed` | Movement speed relative to player |
| `aggro_time` | How long creature stays aggressive |
| `pack_link` | If alerted, nearby same-type creatures also alert |
| `flee_threshold` | HP% below which creature flees |
| `attack_cooldown` | Frames between attacks |
| `attack_pattern` | Melee, ranged, or special |
